4.6 Passing a code with Steganography

4.6 Passing a code with Steganography
Peer: Anonymous student|Due: Wed Jan 8, 2025 11:59pmDue: Wed Jan 8, 2025 11:59pm1/8/2025
Review 2 of 7

 

 

Create an encrypted message.  7 others will try to decrypt it.  Reminder: They will also need access.  

Resources:

https://incoherency.co.uk/image-steganography/# Links to an external site. 

Activity:

Now it’s your turn to use steganography to hide a message in your own pixel picture!

Step 1: Create your message. What do you want to be hidden in your pixel image? Your message should be a minimum of three words in length.

Step 2: Create your algorithm. Use the previous activity’s encryption algorithm as a guide to create your own. 

Do not include the step for hiding your encrypted message in the pixel encoding - you will explore that in the next activity.

Your encryption method must include a minimum of 4 steps. You’ve learned about a variety of different encryption methods - be creative! 

Note: Even though using modulus math would make for extremely secure encryption, we will not want to use it in this case since we’d like the message to be decrypted using reverse engineering.

Step 3: Encrypt your message using your algorithm.

 

When you've been assigned them, complete the 7 decryptions.  IF you cannot decrypt the message explain why.  You must leave a comment for it to count as a peer review.  Your explanation is how you get full credit for the peer review.
